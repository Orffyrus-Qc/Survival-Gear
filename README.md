## Learning path (this project)

1. Start with a **known integrated system** (EcoFlow) so you feel power flow, connectors, and limits.
2. Learn to **name and measure** every major component (panel, controller, battery, inverter, protection).
3. Practice **assembly order** that does not cook controllers or reverse-polarize batteries.
4. Study **mixed-brand** rules: voltage first, chemistry second, brand last.
5. Survey **current U.S. market** portable / DIY solar brands and how each architecture works.
6. Build a **minimal scrap system** with hard safety rules (12 V first, fused, no mystery lithium packs).

## Safety baseline (every module)

- Treat every battery as live energy. Short circuits cause fire and injury.
- Never work on energized high-current DC without fuses/breakers sized for the cable.
- Lead-acid and lithium have **different** charge voltages — wrong charger damages or kills packs.
- Scrap lithium (laptop packs, power-tool packs) is advanced and hazardous; this project steers beginners to **12 V sealed lead-acid (SLA)** or known good LiFePO4 with BMS for DIY.
- Outdoor panels produce voltage in sun even when “not connected” — cover or isolate before wiring.
- This material is educational, not certified electrical training. Local codes and a licensed electrician apply for permanent installs.
