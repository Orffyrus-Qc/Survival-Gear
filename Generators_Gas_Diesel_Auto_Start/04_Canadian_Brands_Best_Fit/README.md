# 04 — Canadian Brands & Best Fit

There are few purely “Canadian-made” consumer generators. **Best fit for Canada** means:

1. **CSA** (or equivalent) listing and legal install path  
2. **Dealer / parts / techs** near you (critical in winter)  
3. Fuel that matches **NG / propane / diesel** logistics here  
4. **Cold-weather** behaviour and enclosure design  
5. Support for **French** manuals/UI where you need it (Québec)  

---

## 1. How to choose (priority order)

```
1. Portable vs standby duty
2. Fuel available at your site (NG / LP / gas / diesel)
3. kW needed for critical loads
4. Auto-start + ATS required? (unattended home)
5. Local certified installer + warranty support
6. Brand reputation / noise / features
```

Brand is **after** fuel and duty — same philosophy as solar mixed-brand rules.

---

## 2. Home standby — brands common in Canada

| Brand | Market position | Best fit when… | Canadian notes |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Generac** | Most installed residential standby volume in NA | You want wide dealer/tech availability, solid feature set, competitive pricing | Easiest to find service in many regions; Guardian-class NG/LP; Wi‑Fi monitoring common |
| **Kohler** (Kohler Energy / Rehlko home energy line) | Premium residential / light commercial | Quieter/premium engineering, long-term ownership | Strong reputation; confirm local certified techs before buying |
| **Cummins** | Premium / commercial DNA | Reliability focus, larger or demanding sites | Excellent industrial pedigree; dealer network more commercial in some areas |
| **Briggs & Stratton** | Residential standby competitor | Quote comparison vs Generac/Kohler | Availability varies by province — check installers |
| **Caterpillar / other industrial** | Commercial diesel | Farm, plant, telecom | Via industrial dealers (e.g. regional Cat dealers) — not typical suburban kit |

### Standby best-fit summary (Canada)

| Situation | Strong default direction |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Suburban home **with natural gas**, wants auto-outage | **Generac or Kohler** 14–22 kW class + ATS — pick by **local installer quality** and quote, not internet fan wars |
| Rural **propane** only | Same brands on **LP**; size tank for multi-day + heat appliances that also use LP |
| Remote / long-run fuel logistics / farm | Consider **diesel** industrial genset + proper ATS (Cummins/Cat/multiquip-class dealers) |
| Budget whole-home marketing claims | Verify **critical load panel** design; “22 kW whole house” fails if you have electric heat |

**Installer > badge:** A well-commissioned Generac beats a neglected Kohler (and vice versa). Ask for: permit handling, cold-weather start battery plan, load calculation, and service response time in storms.

## 3. Portable — brands common in Canada

| Brand | Strength | Best fit |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Honda** | Reliability, resale, real rated output culture | Primary portable, cottage, charging electronics; EU inverter class gold standard |
| **Yamaha** | Quiet, quality inverters | Similar to Honda tier |
| **Westinghouse** | Value dual-fuel inverters | Good storm kit ROI at big-box / online |
| **Generac** | Brand familiarity, dual-fuel portables | If you already like their ecosystem |
| **Champion** | Common big-box watts/$ | Budget portable; maintain carefully |
| **Firman / DuroMax / Pulsar** etc. | Dual/tri-fuel value | OK if you accept mixed QC; verify CSA marks |
| **Predator (Harbor Freight)** | Cheap watts | Less ideal as sole medical/home backup |

### Portable best-fit summary (Canada)

| Situation | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| Quiet electronics + efficiency | **Honda** or **Yamaha** inverter |
| Storm kit dual-fuel on a budget | **Westinghouse / Generac / Champion** dual-fuel (check CSA, real surge ratings) |
| Jobsite / tools | Open-frame conventional; diesel portable if you already run diesel equipment |
| Charge EcoFlow / battery stations | Inverter gen preferred (cleaner power, throttle) |

## 4. “Canadian brand” reality check

| What people want | What exists |
|------------------|-------------|
| Maple-leaf consumer gen brand on every shelf | Rare as a dominant category |
| Canadian **retailers** | Canadian Tire, Home Depot Canada, Rona/Réno-Dépôt, Costco CA, specialized gen dealers |
| Canadian **propane / gas** ecosystem | Provincial gas codes + local propane distributors — this is your real “local brand” partner |
| Canadian **electrical contractors** | The critical partner for ATS/standby |
| Industrial | Wajax, Finning (Cat), Cummins dealers, etc. |

**Best “Canadian fit” is often: North American brand + Canadian certified installer + CSA equipment + fuel contract you can rely on in January.**

---

## 5. Québec-specific notes (Orffyrus-Qc context)

- Winter outages + **verglas** (ice storms): prioritize **auto-start standby** or rock-solid portable plan **before** the storm.  
- **Propane** common outside NG networks.  
- Confirm equipment **CSA** markings; gray-import units can fail inspection.  
- Hire **licensed** electrician / gas tech; RBQ categories apply for much construction work.  
- Cold: battery heaters / quality start batteries, snow clearance around enclosure, LP regulator sizing.  
- French documentation: ask dealer for FR manuals and app language.  
- Hydro-Québec outages: NG often remains available → NG standby shines; still have CO alarms and a backup plan if gas pressure fails.

## 6. Brand matrix by use case

| Use case | Fuel | Auto-start | Brand direction |
|----------|------|------------|-----------------|
| City home, NG, medical/fridge/furnace | NG | **Yes ATS** | Generac / Kohler / Cummins via top local installer |
| Rural home, propane heat | LP | **Yes ATS** | Same; coordinate tank capacity with propane supplier |
| Cottage weekends | Gas or dual-fuel | Manual OK | Honda EU or dual-fuel inverter mid-size |
| Farm multi-day | Diesel | ATS or manual | Industrial diesel + proper transfer gear |
| Apartment / condo | — | Battery PPS | EcoFlow-class (see solar module); gens often banned |
| Hybrid solar + gen | Gas/LP inverter | Manual or smart start | Honda/quality inverter + transfer discipline |

## 7. Shopping checklist (Canada)

- [ ] CSA / approved for Canadian market  
- [ ] Continuous kW at **your** fuel (LP derate!)  
- [ ] 120/240 V if you need 240 V well pumps / dryers  
- [ ] Local parts + tech (ask: “Who services this in my town?”)  
- [ ] Warranty in **Canada** (who honours it)  
- [ ] App monitoring in your language  
- [ ] Enclosure / cold package options  
- [ ] Quiet enough for lot lines  
- [ ] Written load calculation for standby  
- [ ] Permit path clear  

---

## 8. Generac vs Kohler vs Cummins (practical, not religious)

| Factor | Generac | Kohler | Cummins |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| Dealer density (many regions) | Often highest | Good | Good, more commercial in places |
| Price positioning | Value–mid | Premium | Premium |
| Features / Wi‑Fi | Very common | Strong | Strong |
| What actually matters | Installer skill + maintenance | Same | Same |

**Recommendation style for this project:** get **two quotes** (e.g. Generac and Kohler) from installers who will do load calc + cold-climate commissioning. Pick the package with better service SLA, not the glossy brochure.
