# 05 — Cold Climate & Safe Operation (Canada)

Ice storms and −30 °C nights expose weak batteries, bad fuel, and CO mistakes.

---

## 1. Carbon monoxide (non-negotiable)

- Portables: **outdoors only**, far from windows, doors, soffits, heat-recovery intakes.  
- Never in garage with door open — CO still enters living space.  
- Install **CO alarms** on every floor; test monthly.  
- Standby exhaust must meet clearance rules from openings.  
- Symptoms (headache, nausea, confusion) → outdoors + emergency services.

---

## 2. Cold starting — gasoline / propane portables

| Practice | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Fresh fuel + stabilizer | Old gas ruins carbs in storage |
| Correct viscosity oil for temperature | Manual winter grade |
| Battery tender on electric-start models | Cold cuts cranking power |
| Propane mode for storage readiness | No varnish in bowl |
| Cover unit **off engine** after cool-down | Snow/rain protection without trapping heat while running |
| Clear snow from intake/exhaust before start | Prevents overheat and CO redirection |

---

## 3. Cold starting — diesel

- Use **winter diesel** / anti-gel per regional practice  
- Glow plug / grid heater cycles matter — wait the light  
- Block heaters on larger sets when shore power exists  
- Avoid extended idling with no load (wet stacking) — apply real load after warm-up  
- Water in fuel is winter enemy — quality storage tanks

---

## 4. Standby enclosures in snow country

- [ ] Pad level, drainage, not a snow dump zone from roof  
- [ ] Exhaust and intake free of drifts  
- [ ] Service access doors not iced shut  
- [ ] Wi-Fi antenna / module dry  
- [ ] Rodent protection for wiring  
- [ ] Exercise schedule active all winter  

---

## 5. Fuel storage (portable world)

| Fuel | Storage tips |
|------|----------------|
| Gasoline | Approved cans, stabilizer, cool ventilated shed, rotate |
| Propane cylinders | Upright, outdoors/ per code, protect valves, never indoors living space |
| Diesel | Clean dry tank, biocide if storing long, winter blend |

Legal quantity limits exist — follow local fire code.

---

## 6. Electrical safety

- Dry hands, dry connections, outdoor connectors with drip loops  
- Correct cord gauge; no daisy-chained junk cords  
- Inlet box cover closed when not in use  
- Interlock or transfer switch — **always**  
- After flood events: do not energize wet generators or panels

---

## 7. Hearing & neighbours

- Extended open-frame noise causes conflict and fatigue  
- Inverters for night electronics; full power tools by day  
- Standbys are quieter but still audible — siting matters

---

## 8. Seasonal maintenance calendar (simple)

| Season | Actions |
|--------|---------|
| Fall | Oil change if due, fuel plan, battery test, exercise verify, CO alarm batteries |
| Winter | Snow clearance, LP levels, post-storm inspection |
| Spring | Full service hours check, rodent damage, clean enclosure |
| Summer | Storage discipline for portables; don’t forget exercise on standby |

---

## 9. Outage go-order (human procedure)

1. Safety: CO, gas smells, downed lines → stay back.  
2. If standby: confirm auto sequence / silence nuisance alarms only if you understand them.  
3. If portable: outdoor placement → start → inlet/interlock → critical loads only.  
4. Keep fridge closed; prioritize sump / medical / heat control.  
5. Conserve fuel first 24 h until storm duration is known.  
6. When utility returns: transfer back, cool down, refuel plan, note faults.
